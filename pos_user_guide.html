System Overview

Understand what the POS system is, what it can do, and how to launch it on your computer.

What is the POS System?

The Premium POS System is a powerful, all-in-one Point of Sale and business management application designed specifically for shops. It runs entirely in your web browser — no internet connection, no complicated installation, and no ongoing subscription fees. Everything you need to run your business is in one single file on your computer.

The system stores all data securely in your browser's local storage, meaning your inventory, invoices, and customer records are always available, even without the internet.

Key Features at a Glance
🧾

Point of Sale

Create, save & print tax invoices in seconds

📦

Inventory

Manage stock with Goods Received Notes and purchase returns

📊

Reports

12+ business reports including Profit & Loss, Tax Liability, and more

🏦

Accounts Payable

Track what you owe to suppliers with credit management

💳

Accounts Receivable

Track customer credit balances and outstanding payments

🔒

User Security

Multi-user access with role-based permissions

How to Launch the System

There are two simple ways to start the POS system on a Windows computer:

A
Quick Method (Recommended): Double-click run.bat

Locate the run.bat file in your POS folder and double-click it. The system will instantly open in your default browser. This is the fastest way to get started.

B
Manual Method: Open index.html in a browser

Open the index.html file directly in Google Chrome, Microsoft Edge, Firefox, or Safari. You can drag-and-drop the file into a browser window.

💡
Best Browser Recommendation

For the best experience, use Google Chrome or Microsoft Edge. These browsers offer the most reliable local storage and printing support.

Creating Invoices (Point of Sale)

Learn how to create and print a sales invoice step by step.

Opening a New Invoice

There are two ways to start a new invoice:

  • Click the New Invoice quick action button on the Dashboard
  • Click Point of Sale in the left sidebar, then click New Invoice
Figure 3.1 — The New Invoice form. A unique invoice number is automatically assigned.

The system automatically assigns a sequential invoice number in the format INV-YYYYMMDD-001 and sets today's date. You do not need to fill these in manually.

Step-by-Step: Creating an Invoice
1
Select the Customer

Use the Customer Name dropdown to choose a registered customer, or leave it as Walk-in Customer for a cash sale with no customer record required.

2
Choose Payment Terms

Select Cash for immediate payment or Credit if the customer will pay later. Credit sales are automatically tracked in Accounts Receivable.

3
Tax Registered (Optional)

If the customer is a VAT-registered business, tick the Tax Registered checkbox. This will automatically add 18% VAT to the invoice total and format it as a proper Tax Invoice.

4
Search and Add Items

Click the part search field and type the part number or part name. A dropdown will appear with matching items. Click on an item to select it. Then enter the Qty (quantity), verify the Price, and enter any item-level Discount. Press Enter or click Add (Enter) to add it to the invoice.

5
Repeat for More Items

The item is added to the invoice table. Repeat Step 4 to add more items. You can remove any item by clicking the delete button (✕) next to it in the table.

Saving & Printing the Invoice

Inventory Management

Manage your stock levels by recording goods received from suppliers and processing purchase returns.

Receiving Stock (Goods Received Note – GRN)

Every time you receive goods from a supplier, you must create a Goods Received Note (GRN). This increases your stock levels and records what you owe to the supplier.

Navigate to Inventory → Stock In/Out to open the GRN form.

Figure 4.1 — The Goods Received Note (GRN) form for recording incoming stock from suppliers
1
Select the Supplier

Choose the supplier from the Supplier dropdown. Suppliers must be pre-registered under Settings → Creditors.

2
Enter Supplier Invoice Reference

Type the supplier's own invoice number in the Supplier Invoice / Ref No. field (e.g., GS-9022). This is used for cross-referencing.

3
Set Payment Terms

Select Credit if you will pay the supplier later (creates an Accounts Payable record), or Cash if paid immediately.

4
Add Items Received

Search for each received item by part number or name. Enter the Qty received, the Unit Cost paid, and any supplier Discount. Click Add (Enter) to add each item to the GRN table.

5
Enter Bill Discount (if any) and Save

If the supplier gave an overall discount on the bill, enter it in Bill Discount. Then click Save & Receive Stock. Stock quantities are immediately updated across the entire system and the selling price is updated if the cost changed.

📦
GRN History

All saved GRNs are listed under Inventory → GRN History. Click any GRN to view its items, payment status, and to record supplier payments.

Purchase Returns

If you need to return goods to a supplier (e.g., damaged items), go to Inventory → Purchase Returns. Select the original GRN and mark the items being returned. Stock levels and supplier balances are automatically recalculated.

Settings & Data Management

Configure your master records — customers, suppliers, items, and users — and protect your data.

The Settings / Management Panel

Click Settings in the left sidebar to open the Management Panel. This is where you add and maintain all the core data that the POS system uses. The panel has four tabs:

Figure 6.1 — The Settings panel showing the Debtors management tab with Add, Edit, and Delete actions
👥

Debtors (Customers)

Add customers who buy on credit. Set their Name, Phone, Address, and Credit Limit. Customers registered here appear in the invoice dropdown. Their outstanding balance is tracked automatically.

🏭

Creditors (Suppliers)

Add your suppliers here. Enter their Name, Phone, Address, and Payment Terms. Registered creditors appear in the GRN supplier dropdown. Their balances are tracked automatically.

📋

Items (Products)

Manage your product master list. Each item has a Part No, Name, initial Stock Qty, Unit Cost, and Selling Price. Items added here appear in all invoice and GRN searches.

🔐

Users

Add and manage staff accounts. Each user has a Username, Password, and Role (Admin or Staff). Admin users have full access; Staff users have restricted access.

How to Add a New Record
1
Click the appropriate tab (Debtors, Creditors, Items, or Users) to switch to that section
2
Click the "+ Add New" button at the top right — a form modal will appear
3
Fill in all required fields in the form and click Save Record
✏️
Editing & Deleting Records

Click the Edit button next to any record to modify its details. Click Delete to permanently remove it. Note: you cannot delete a record that is currently linked to existing invoices or GRNs — the system will warn you.

Chapter 6 · Backup & Restore
💾 Backup & Restore Your Data

Since all data is stored in your browser's local storage, it is essential to regularly back up your data. If you clear your browser history, switch computers, or the browser's storage is accidentally cleared, you will lose your data unless you have a backup file.

Figure 6.2 — The Backup & Restore dialog showing the Download and Restore options
⬇️

How to Create a Backup

  1. Go to Dashboard
  2. Click Backup & Restore button
  3. Click Download Backup
  4. A file named pos-backup-[date].json is saved to your Downloads folder
  5. Store this file safely (USB drive, cloud storage)
⬆️

How to Restore from Backup

  1. Go to Dashboard
  2. Click Backup & Restore button
  3. Click Choose File in the Restore section
  4. Select your .json backup file
  5. Click Restore from File
🚨
Critical Warning: Never Clear Browser Data Without a Backup!

Restoring a backup will completely overwrite all current data. Always confirm you have the correct backup file before restoring. Backup frequently — we recommend at least once per day at end of business.

Recommended Backup Schedule
FrequencyWho Should Do ThisStorage Location
Daily (end of business)All shopsUSB flash drive or Google Drive
WeeklyShops with high transaction volumeExternal hard drive
Before major changesAll shopsAny safe location
Before browser updatesAll shopsAny safe location
🌐
Moving to a New Computer

To move your entire POS system to a new computer: (1) Create a backup on the old computer, (2) Copy the entire POS folder to the new computer, (3) Open the system on the new computer, (4) Go to Backup & Restore and restore your backup file. All your data will be fully restored.
